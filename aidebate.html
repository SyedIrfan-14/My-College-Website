AI Debate

An AI debate involves a structured and often spirited discussion on various aspects of artificial intelligence (AI), its impacts, ethics, implications, and future developments

AI Debate Rules and Regulations:

1. Topic Selection and Preparation - Topics will be provided on the spot. - Participants are allowed to use AI tools for research and preparation within the given time limit.

2. Opening Statements - Each participant's opening statement is limited to a maximum of 2 minutes.

3. Rebuttals - The rebuttal phase will last for one hour. - Participants can engage in back-and-forth discussions during this time.

4. Cross Examination - Cross examination will occur after the rebuttal phase and will be limited to 10 minutes.

5. Closing Statements - The maximum time limit for closing statements is 2 minutes per participant.

6. Internet Usage - The use of the internet is prohibited once the debate starts.

Judging Criteria

- Judges will evaluate participants based on the following criteria: 1. Clarity and Presentation: The clarity of arguments and the ability to present them effectively. 2. Logical Reasoning: The use of sound logic and evidence to support arguments. 3. Relevance: The relevance of arguments and points to the given topic. 4. Respectful Engagement: The ability to engage in respectful and constructive debates. 5. Counter-arguments: The effectiveness of counter-arguments during rebuttals. 6. Cross Examination: Performance during the cross-examination phase. 7. Closing Statements: The strength and impact of closing statements. 8. Adherence to Rules: Participants' adherence to the established debate rules and time limits.
