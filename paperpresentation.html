AI Tek Thesis

An AI Tek Thesis typically refers to the act of delivering a presentation about a research paper or study related to artificial intelligence (AI). It's a common practice in academic and professional settings where researchers, students, and experts share their findings, insights, and innovations with a wider audience. AI paper presentations are commonly seen in conferences, workshops, seminars, and other events dedicated to AI research and development.

RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR AI TEK THESIS

1. Topic Assignment: a. Participants will be randomly assigned topics just before their presentation. b. Participants will be given some amount of time to prepare their presentation.

2. AI Tool Usage: Participants are allowed to use AI tools like tome.ai and slidesgo.com for their presentations.

3. Time Limits: a. Each participant will have a specified time limit for their presentation (e.g., 5-10 minutes). b. Exceeding the time limit will result in penalties or disqualification.

4. Presentation Format: a. Presentations must be in a digital format, such as slides or a video. b. The format should be easily accessible and viewable by the judges and audience.

5. Original Content: a. Presentations should primarily consist of original content created by the participants. b. Proper citations and references must be provided for any external content used.

6. Equipment and Connectivity: a. Participants are responsible for ensuring the functionality of their devices and internet connectivity. b. Technical difficulties will not result in additional time for the presentation.

7. Judging Criteria: a. Presentations will be judged based on content, creativity, clarity, and relevance to the assigned topic. b. Judges may consider the effective use of AI tools in the evaluation.

8. Fair Play: a. Participants are expected to conduct themselves with fairness and respect toward other participants and judges. b. Any form of cheating or disruptive behavior will result in disqualification.

9.Decisions of Judges: a. The decisions of the judges are final and binding. b. Judges may provide feedback to participants after the competition.

10.Privacy: a. Participants should respect privacy and confidentiality when using AI tools and handling data.
